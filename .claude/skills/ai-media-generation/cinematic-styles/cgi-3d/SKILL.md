## Primary Behavioral Framework

### Material Realism Principle (Maxim's synthesis of perception research)

Viewer trust scales with material fidelity. Every surface in a render has declared PBR properties (albedo, metalness, roughness, normal, displacement). Wrong materials trigger the uncanny-valley response within 200ms — viewer flags "fake" pre-consciously even if they can't articulate why.

**Mechanism:**
- Brain spent 200,000 years evaluating material properties (is this food safe? is this tool strong?)
- Material perception bypasses conscious analysis — direct limbic response
- Correct physical behavior (subsurface scattering on skin, caustics on glass, fresnel on metal) creates *embodied trust*
- Trust transfers from render accuracy to brand accuracy

### Endowment Effect (Thaler, 1980)

Once a person imagines owning something, the imagined-loss of not having it becomes 2-3× stronger than gain of having it. 3D renders that immerse the viewer in product detail trigger pre-ownership mental simulation.

**Application:**
- Macro detail shots create "touching the product mentally" sensation
- Orbit sequences let viewer "examine" the product as if holding it
- Contextual integration shots (product in viewer's imagined environment) cement ownership feeling
- Post-orbit close on key feature gives "I can see myself with this" moment

### Combined mechanism

Material Realism establishes the product as REAL (trust prerequisite). Endowment Effect establishes the product as YOURS (commitment to purchase). The viewer leaves with embodied trust + pre-ownership feeling = purchase confidence.

## The 4-Act Structure (framework-driven)

### Act 1 — Material Declaration (0-2s) — activates Material Realism
Opening: product appears in studio context. First shot is material-heavy — macro surface, texture, light-on-surface. Viewer's limbic system locks onto material reality.

### Act 2 — Product Establishing (2-5s) — activates Photorealism Trust
Wider shot: full product in studio. HDRI dome lighting. Museum-like framing. Anchoring happens here.

### Act 3 — Examination Orbit (5-10s) — activates Endowment Effect
Slow orbit. Viewer sees product from multiple angles. Mental simulation of "picking it up."

### Act 4 — Hero Close + Spec (10-15s) — activates Scarcity + purchase confidence
Return to macro detail on hero feature. Product spec overlay. Brand mark. Viewer leaves with embodied sense of "I've seen this real thing."

## Sales / Lead-Gen Templates

### Template 1 — Luxury watch launch (15s) — optimizing for premium purchase confidence

```
Path-traced 3D render, 15 seconds, luxury positioning. V-Ray / Octane
equivalent quality.

ACT 1 — Material Declaration (0-2s):
Extreme macro on watch dial surface. PBR metalness 0.95, roughness 0.05
on polished steel case. Anisotropic brushed pattern on sub-dial. Slight
environment reflection from HDRI. 4K surface detail.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Material Realism triggers within 400ms. Viewer's
limbic system accepts watch as real.

ACT 2 — Product Establishing (2-5s):
Pull out to medium shot of full watch on dark velvet surface. HDRI studio
lighting — soft-box key from upper-left, rim light from behind, fill at
30% key. Watch centered on rule-of-thirds intersection. Museum grade.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Photorealism Trust established. Anchoring Effect
primes "this is expensive."

ACT 3 — Examination Orbit (5-11s):
Slow orbit clockwise 360° around watch over 6 seconds. Constant angular
velocity. Maintain focus on watch center. Sub-dial complications visible
from 120°. Case-back engraving visible from 240°.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Endowment Effect — viewer mentally picks up watch.
Pre-ownership imagery forms.

ACT 4 — Hero Close + Spec (11-15s):
Return to hero angle (15° off-axis, slight upward tilt to emphasize scale).
Macro push-in to bezel detail. Spec overlay slides in: "[MODEL] · [LIMITED
EDITION 247/500] · [PRICE]". Brand logo top-left.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Scarcity Signaling (limited edition number). Anchoring
(price AFTER premium positioning). Logo lock-up encodes brand with trust.
```

### Template 2 — Architectural visualization for pre-sale (30s)

```
Pre-construction real estate architectural visualization, 30 seconds,

ACT 4 — Signature Moment (25-30s):
Hero shot of signature architectural feature (staircase, window system,
ceiling detail). Listing price + availability overlay. Agent contact.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Scarcity + price anchor + purchase path activation.
```

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Behavioral mechanism it breaks |
|---|---|
| Unrealistic material physics (wrong reflections, wrong shadows) | Breaks Material Realism → uncanny valley → TRUST LOST |
| Too-fast orbit (<3s per 90°) | Breaks Endowment Effect (no time to mentally examine) |
| Harsh direct lighting (flat look) | Breaks Photorealism Trust + breaks premium Anchoring |
| Price BEFORE premium positioning | Breaks Anchoring (sets wrong reference point) |
| Generic product angle (0° straight-on) | Breaks Scarcity Signaling (looks like catalog, not museum) |
| No macro detail shots | Breaks Material Realism opening + breaks Endowment Effect examination |
