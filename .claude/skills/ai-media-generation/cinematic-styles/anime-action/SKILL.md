## Primary Behavioral Framework

### Social Identity Theory (Henri Tajfel & John Turner, 1979)

Humans derive part of identity from group membership. Brand loyalty in youth markets operates largely through social identity — "I am the kind of person who uses X" becomes identity component.

**Mechanism:**
- In-group signals (specific aesthetic, vocabulary, references) trigger belonging
- Out-group contrast (not-for-everyone signaling) strengthens in-group bond
- Anime aesthetic carries strong in-group signaling in Gen-Z / gaming markets
- Brand presence in tribe-coded content encodes brand AS tribe-member

### Pattern Interruption (within an aesthetic category)

Anime aesthetic operates in a saturated category. Pattern interruption here isn't about being weird — it's about being more authentically in-group than others.

**Application:**
- Specific studio-style references (Ufotable, MAPPA, Trigger aesthetic cues)
- Correct sub-cultural vocabulary (ahegao, chuunibyou, gundam poses used accurately)
- Avoiding outsider-signaling tells (awkward proportions, wrong speed-line direction)
- Authentic tribe-member content signals get rewarded with engagement

### Combined mechanism

Social Identity Theory establishes brand as in-group. Pattern Interruption within the aesthetic ensures the brand is PERCEIVED AS AUTHENTIC to the tribe rather than outsider-trying-to-fit-in. Youth audiences have finely-tuned outsider detection; inauthenticity kills.

## The 4-Act Structure (framework-driven)

### Act 1 — Tribe Signal (0-2s) — activates Pattern Interruption + Social Identity
Opening frame must read as authentically anime to insiders. Speed lines, character silhouette, correct aesthetic markers.

### Act 2 — Protagonist Positioning (2-5s) — activates Aspirational Self
Hero character (aspirational figure) established. Clear archetype readable. Pose signals identity alignment.

### Act 3 — Peak Action (5-8s) — activates Emotional Amplification + in-group markers
Signature action moment. Power-up, transformation, or technique reveal. Emotional peak amplified through anime conventions.

### Act 4 — Brand-as-Tribe (8-10s) — activates Social Identity encoding
Brand revealed in tribe-authentic context. Brand mark integrated into aesthetic (not bolted on as commercial).
