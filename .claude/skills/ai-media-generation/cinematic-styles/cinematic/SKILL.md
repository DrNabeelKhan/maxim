## Primary Behavioral Framework

### Hero's Journey (Joseph Campbell, 1949)

The mythological narrative structure that maps universally to human meaning-making: Ordinary World → Call to Adventure → Refusal / Trials → Mentor/Guide → Transformation → Return with Gift.

**Why it converts viewers to advocates:**
- Brain evolved for narrative — it processes story 22× more memorably than facts (Jennifer Aaker, Stanford)
- The Hero arc triggers identification — viewer maps themselves onto protagonist
- Transformation moment produces catharsis → emotional encoding of brand as "catalyst for change"
- Return frame prompts behavior: viewers who identified with protagonist act consistent with their new self-image

### Peak-End Rule (Daniel Kahneman, 1993)

Viewers remember two moments disproportionately: the emotional peak and the ending. An hour-long experience rated by a 2-second peak + 2-second close.

**Application to cinematic video:**
- Engineer the emotional peak at 70-80% of runtime (not at the end — the ending is for resolution)
- Make the peak VISUALLY maximal: widest shot, warmest light, highest movement, most color
- Make the ending RESONANT not climactic — a single image or statement that carries the film's meaning forward into the viewer's memory

### Combined mechanism

Hero's Journey creates the commitment substrate (viewer identifies, experiences transformation). Peak-End Rule stamps the memory (peak = emotional climax; end = resonant carry-forward). The viewer leaves the experience with a compressed memory that says: *"I am a person who identifies with [BRAND]."* That's commitment.

## Supporting Frameworks

- **Emotional Arc Theory** (Vonnegut / Reagan-Riedl): 6 canonical emotional arcs; Rags-to-Riches and Man-in-a-Hole perform best for brand content
- **Mirror Neuron Theory**: close-ups of protagonist emotion trigger viewer physiological response (3× emotional transfer vs. wide shots)
- **Narrative Transportation** (Green & Brock): viewers immersed in narrative lower cognitive defenses; brand claims accepted without counter-argument

## Behavioral → Cinematic Translation

Every beat below is chosen because it activates a specific behavioral mechanism:

| Cinematic beat | Behavioral mechanism activated |
|---|---|
| Extreme close-up on protagonist eye (opening) | Mirror neuron priming — viewer begins emotional synchronization within 400ms |
| Slow establishing wide shot | Narrative Transportation onset — viewer accepts story world, defenses lower |
| Verité handheld during trials | Authenticity signal — Hero's Journey "trial" phase feels real, not scripted |
| Color desaturation during pre-transformation | Visual metaphor for character state; primes contrast for peak |
| Warmest/widest/slowest shot at 70-80% runtime | Peak-End Rule engineering — this IS the peak, engineered maximally |
| Static hold on final resonant image | Memory encoding — longer hold = deeper memory trace |
| Silent outro before brand card | Catharsis window — viewer processes emotion before brand association locks in |

## The 5-Act Structure (framework-driven, not aesthetic-driven)

### Act 1 — Identification (0-15% runtime) — activates Mirror Neurons + Narrative Transportation
Protagonist established. Close-up emotional framing. Viewer begins mapping self onto protagonist. Verité aesthetic to signal authenticity.

### Act 2 — Call + Refusal (15-30%) — activates Hero's Journey beat 1+2
Inciting moment. The protagonist's world shifts. Rising tension. Color palette narrows. Viewer is now emotionally invested.

### Act 3 — Trials + Guide (30-65%) — activates Hero's Journey beat 3+4
Protagonist's struggle. Brand appears as guide (NOT hero — per StoryBrand; see style 12). Montage-friendly. Varied pacing.

### Act 4 — Transformation Peak (65-85%) — activates Peak-End Rule (peak)
The emotional climax. Visually maximal. Slow-motion on expression moments. Widest shots. Warmest light. This is the Peak.

### Act 5 — Resonance (85-100%) — activates Peak-End Rule (end) + Behavioral Encoding
Protagonist's new normal. Brief. Affirming. Brand as memorable final frame. Call to viewer to begin their own story.

## Sales / Lead-Gen Templates (behavioral-engineered)

### Template 1 — Founder origin brand film (2:00) — optimizing for commitment + investor resonance

```
Cinematic founder origin film, 2:00 runtime, Hero's Journey structure,
Peak-End engineered.

ACT 1 — Identification (0:00-0:18):
Extreme close-up on [FOUNDER]'s hands holding [OBJECT tied to origin moment].
Natural light, shallow DOF. Face partially visible. Voice (introspective,
first-person, past tense): "Five years ago, I didn't know this would become
anything."
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Mirror neurons engage within 400ms via hand close-up.
Past-tense narration creates Narrative Transportation — viewer enters story world.

ACT 2 — Call + Refusal (0:18-0:36):
Wide reveal of location — modest workspace, early-stage environment. Color
desaturated, cool-grey grade. Voice: "I saw [PROBLEM]. I knew someone had
to solve it. I didn't know it had to be me."
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Refusal-of-call beat deepens protagonist identification.
Viewer thinks: "I've also had a moment when I could have acted and didn't."

ACT 3 — Trials + Guide (0:36-1:18):
Montage — six 4-second beats showing prototypes failing, long nights, doubt,
and incremental progress. Verité handheld. Voice narrates key insights as
each trial ends.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Trials phase sustains engagement via variable reward
pattern (each beat delivers different emotional micro-payoff). Brand/product
visible in background — guide-not-hero role.

ACT 4 — Transformation Peak (1:18-1:42):
PEAK ENGINEERING — widest shot of entire film, warmest grade of entire film,
slowest motion of entire film. [FOUNDER] watches first customer / team / 
community using product. Face catches golden light. 24-second hold.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Peak-End Rule activation. This is the 2-second
compressed memory viewer will retain 12 months later. Engineered to be
visually unforgettable.

ACT 5 — Resonance (1:42-2:00):
Return to close-up of [FOUNDER], now looking to camera. Voice: "This story
isn't about me anymore." Pause 2 seconds. "If you've ever thought about
[CALL ALIGNED TO VIEWER], start." Hard cut to brand wordmark on clean
background. Hold 3 seconds. Silence.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Narrative transfer — viewer becomes protagonist. Silent
outro creates catharsis window where brand association locks into memory.
```

### Template 2 — Mission-driven brand anthem (90s) — optimizing for cultural alignment + conversion

```
Brand anthem, 90 seconds, Emotional Arc = Man-in-a-Hole (fall-then-rise),
Peak at 70s.

ACT 1 — Problem Reality (0-15s):
Rapid montage of the problem the brand exists to solve. Real-world footage,
high contrast, urgent pacing. Voiceover asks the provocative question.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Emotional Arc "fall" — viewer experiences the problem
viscerally. Stakes established.

ACT 2 — The Counter-Response (15-35s):
Introduce what if. Brand concept. Pacing slows. Music swells. Hope enters.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Emotional Arc turn — viewer experiences relief of
possibility.

ACT 3 — Brand In Action (35-60s):
Montage of brand's real work, real customers, real impact. Accumulation.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Social Proof + narrative evidence stack.

ACT 4 — Peak (60-75s):
Single resonant image of the brand's vision realized. Widest, warmest,
slowest shot. Music climax. Hold longer than expected — 8 seconds.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Peak-End Rule peak. Engineered for maximum memory.

ACT 5 — Call (75-90s):
"Join us" CTA. Brand wordmark + tagline. URL. Music resolves. Fade.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Behavioral Activation — viewer's emotional state now
primed for action. Conversion rate 2-3× vs. cold CTA.
```

## Anti-Patterns (kill the behavioral mechanism)

| Anti-pattern | Behavioral mechanism it breaks |
|---|---|
| Brand as protagonist | Breaks StoryBrand + Mirror Neurons (viewer can't identify with a brand) |
| Peak moved to final frame | Breaks Peak-End Rule (peak and end should be separate beats) |
| Generic "founder working late" montage | Breaks Narrative Transportation (viewer checks out) |
| Mission statement spoken aloud | Breaks show-don't-tell — narrative transportation requires inference |
| Even pacing throughout | No peak = no Peak-End encoding = forgotten film |
| Cut-to-black hard ending | Breaks catharsis window — brand association doesn't lock in |
| Music constant at same intensity | Breaks Emotional Arc — viewer doesn't experience the journey |
