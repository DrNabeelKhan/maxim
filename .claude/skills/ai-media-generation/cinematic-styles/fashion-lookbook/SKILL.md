## Primary Behavioral Framework

### Aesthetic Resonance

Fashion is art-adjacent. Viewers evaluate fashion content on pure aesthetic response within 300ms — before analytical assessment. Technical craft in composition, lighting, movement, color is non-negotiable because it IS the communication.

**Mechanism:**
- Aesthetic quality processed pre-consciously in limbic system
- Poor aesthetic quality → brand perceived as lower-tier (regardless of actual quality)
- High aesthetic quality → halo effect transfers to product quality perception
- Aesthetic resonance creates emotional investment that anchors price tolerance

### Aspirational Self (Higgins, 1987 — Self-Discrepancy Theory)

Humans carry mental models of: actual self (current), ought self (what we should be), and ideal self (what we wish we were). Fashion content most effectively activates the ideal-self model.

**Application:**
- Models represent viewer's ideal-self rather than actual-self (aspirational > relatable)
- Environments represent aspirational settings (architectural spaces, travel destinations, curated interiors)
- Time of day suggests abundance (golden hour > harsh noon)
- Body language suggests confidence + effortless grace
- Product becomes the bridge between current-self and ideal-self

### Social Comparison Theory (Leon Festinger, 1954)

Humans evaluate themselves by comparison to others. "Upward social comparison" (comparing to people perceived as better) can either motivate (I want to be like them) or depress (I feel inadequate). Fashion content must trigger motivating upward comparison.

**Application:**
- Models are attainable-aspirational (not unattainably perfect)
- Product visible as the "achievable" element
- Message: "with this product, you're in their league"
- Avoiding unattainability cues (avoid creating alienation)

### Combined mechanism

Aesthetic Resonance establishes premium quality signal. Aspirational Self pulls viewer toward ideal-self version via model/environment. Social Comparison makes the ideal-self feel achievable via the product. Result: viewer leaves with status-aligned desire for the product.

## The 4-Part Fashion Structure

### 1. Textural Opening (0-3s) — activates Aesthetic Resonance + Scarcity
Extreme close-up on garment texture, embellishment, or fabric movement. No faces. No wide shots. Build tactile desire.

### 2. Figure Reveal (3-10s) — activates Aspirational Self
Slow reveal of model. Detail → wider → full figure. Deliberate movement.

### 3. Editorial Moment (10-20s) — activates Peak-End Rule peak + Social Comparison
The hero image. Magazine cover moment. Longest hold. Pure visual impact.

### 4. Brand Signature (20-30s+) — activates Scarcity + Peak-End end
Designer logo / collection title. Environmentally integrated.

## Sales / Lead-Gen Templates

### Template 1 — Haute couture collection launch 45s (optimizing for aspirational premium purchase)

```
Haute couture collection launch, 45 seconds, anamorphic 2.39:1 letterbox,
natural light dominant, 35mm film grain. Editorial cinematic.

ACT 1 — Textural Opening (0-3s):
Extreme close-up on [KEY FABRIC DETAIL — beadwork, silk sheen, tailored
seam]. Shallow DOF f/1.4, soft natural window light, subtle fabric
movement suggesting breath or air. No face. No context. Just material
excellence.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Aesthetic Resonance primed at the perception-limit.
Within 400ms, viewer's limbic system evaluates "premium quality."
Scarcity Signaling (museum-like presentation, no ad clutter).

ACT 2 — Figure Reveal (3-15s):
Slow pull-out from detail to medium shot of [MODEL] — face still obscured
or in profile. Model stands in atelier-like interior, single window light
source. Subtle gestural movement — turn of wrist, tilt of head. 12-second
long take.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Aspirational Self onset. Model's partial face allows
viewer to project themselves. Environment (atelier) signals exclusivity.
Slow Reveal rewards patience = deepens aesthetic processing.

ACT 3 — Editorial Moment (15-30s):
Full figure reveal, environmental framing. Architectural space, composition
balanced around model. Static or minimal camera movement. This is the
"magazine cover" — hold 15 seconds at full figure. Let viewer look. Let
texture of garment fill the frame naturally.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Peak-End Rule peak engineering — 15-second hold
cements the image as memorable. Social Comparison — viewer compares self
to aspirational figure, motivated by achievability via garment purchase.

ACT 4 — Brand Signature (30-45s):
Model exits frame, camera holds on empty space. Collection title etched
into wall, carved into stone, or printed on mailer emerging from frame.
Brand logo integrated environmentally. Slow fade.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Scarcity Signaling (no hard-sell CTA). Peak-End Rule
end (resonant frame carries memory forward). Purchase intent activated
without commercial pushiness.
```

### Template 2 — Fashion e-commerce lookbook 30s (multiple looks)

```
Fashion lookbook 30s, editorial polish, multiple looks in one film.

ACT 1 — Textural Opening (0-3s): flatlay of collection — 5 key pieces on
neutral backdrop. Top-down camera push-in. Natural light. Aesthetic
anticipation.

ACT 2 — 3 Model Reveals (3-24s): sequential looks, each 7 seconds:
  - Look 1: [MODEL 1] in environment. Subtle walk, 90° turn.
  - Look 2: match-cut to [MODEL 2] different environment (same palette).
  - Look 3: [MODEL 3] third environment. Palette family maintained.

ACT 3 — Close (24-30s): back to opening flatlay, re-arranged into grid
showing looks. Collection name. URL.
```

### Template 3 — Designer atelier profile 60s

```
Designer atelier profile, 60 seconds, documentary aesthetic, craft-focused.

ACT 1 — Craft (0-10s): close-up on designer's hands working. Single
light source. Ambient only. Trust the audience.

ACT 2 — Philosophy (10-25s): designer voiceover over atelier footage.
Intimate, unhurried.

ACT 3 — Fashion Moment (25-45s): transition to finished collection —
model in the space, wearing piece we saw being made. Connects craft
to outcome.

ACT 4 — Close (45-60s): designer observing model. Subtle smile.
Collection + designer signature. Integrated environmentally.
```
