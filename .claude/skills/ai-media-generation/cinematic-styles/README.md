## 0. Maxim's Moat — Why This Is Different

Every other prompt library in 2026 sells **aesthetic templates.** "Here's how to write a cinematic prompt." "Here's how to make a TikTok."

Maxim sells **behavioral frameworks translated into cinematic video for specific business outcomes.** Every one of the 15 styles below is engineered from a primary behavioral framework (Fogg, AIDA, Hook Model, Peak-End Rule, Mirror Neurons, Endowment Effect, etc.) down into the specific cinematic structure that activates that framework.

This is the moat. No one else ties behavioral science to video generation at this resolution. A developer can reproduce a "cinematic prompt" in an afternoon. Reproducing Maxim's behavioral-to-cinematic translation requires behavioral psychology expertise + cinema craft + commercial marketing intuition. That's why it's defensible.

**The formula every Maxim cinematic style follows:**

```
Business Outcome  (sales / lead gen / activation / retention / awareness)
       ↓
Primary Behavioral Framework  (the mechanism that moves people to act)
       ↓
Cinematic Structure  (the beats, timing, shots that activate the framework)
       ↓
Prompt Templates  (model-agnostic text that generates video at that frame)
       ↓
Tagged Output  (confidence, applied frameworks, intended outcome)
```

## 2. The Behavioral-Cinematic Translation Method

Every style file follows this unified structure so the moat is evident and reproducible:

```
---
YAML frontmatter with business_outcome + primary_framework
---

# Style Name — [Business Outcome] via [Primary Framework]

## The Maxim Moat
One paragraph: what behavioral framework drives this style + why
reproducing Maxim's behavioral-cinematic translation is a high bar.

## Business Outcome
Specific metric this style moves: purchase rate, follow rate, dwell time,
add-to-cart rate, consultation booking, brand recall, etc.

## Primary Behavioral Framework
The ONE framework that does the heavy lifting. Deep treatment —
mechanism, academic source, application to video.

## Supporting Frameworks
2-3 frameworks that reinforce. Brief treatment.

## Behavioral → Cinematic Translation
How the framework's psychological mechanism maps to specific cinematic
beats. This is where the moat lives.

## The Structure (framework-driven, not aesthetic-driven)
4-5 beats, each named for its behavioral function.

## Sales / Lead-Gen Templates
Model-agnostic templates engineered for business outcomes, not
just aesthetics. Every template specifies what behavioral lever it pulls
at each timestamp.

## Anti-Patterns (what kills the behavioral mechanism)
Common marketing mistakes that neutralize the framework.

## Confidence Tagging + Maxim Integration
How Maxim tags outputs for confidence, applied frameworks, and intended
outcome. How Pack 2 Behavioral Intelligence deepens this style.

## Model Portability
Which video models best support the behavioral-cinematic requirements.
```
