## Primary Behavioral Framework — The 5-Stack

### 1. AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
The skeletal structure. 4-act progression maps cleanly to runtime.

### 2. Jobs-to-be-Done (Clayton Christensen, 2003)
Show the product doing the CUSTOMER'S job, not just existing. Viewers buy hired outcomes, not features.

**Application:**
- Hero shot shows product IN USE contextually (hand holding, mouth tasting, body wearing, home using)
- Benefits framed as "jobs done" not "features"
- Customer perspective dominates

### 3. Loss Aversion (Kahneman & Tversky, 1979)
Loss hurts 2-3× more than gain feels good. Urgency + limited-time framing leverage this.

**Application:**
- "Ends tonight" / "Last X left"
- Before/after framing (what you LOSE by not buying)
- Unit-of-time framing ("saves 3 hours/week you're losing now")

### 4. Scarcity Principle (Cialdini)
Limited availability increases desirability. Both real scarcity (limited stock) and social scarcity (others buying) work.

**Application:**
- Quantity indicators ("247 left")
- Time indicators ("48-hour flash")
- Social proof of depletion ("12 sold in the last hour")

### 5. Anchoring Effect (Kahneman)
First price seen becomes reference. Establish premium anchor BEFORE showing discounted price.

**Application:**
- Original price ($149) flashes first
- Discounted price ($79) shown against anchor
- Savings ("$70 off") becomes salient loss-if-you-don't-buy

### Combined mechanism

Stacked correctly, this 5-framework sequence is nearly unbeatable for direct-response commerce. AIDA moves through the funnel. JTBD frames value as outcome. Loss Aversion creates urgency. Scarcity creates exclusivity. Anchoring establishes value. Viewer arrives at CTA with emotional urgency + clear value + cognitive ease.

## The 4-Act Structure (AIDA + 5-stack)

### Act 1 — Attention (0-2s)
Product hero shot. Idealized lighting. Viewer recognizes product. Pattern interruption in feed.

### Act 2 — Interest via JTBD (2-5s)
Customer context use. Product doing the customer's job. Relatable scenario.

### Act 3 — Desire via Stack (5-12s)
Benefit stat (Anchoring). Peak moment (Peak-End). Micro-social-proof (Social Proof).

### Act 4 — Action via Urgency (12-15s)
Anchored price reveal. Scarcity indicator. Single CTA. URL.

## Sales / Lead-Gen Templates (the commercial workhorses)

### Template 1 — DTC product 15s conversion ad (optimized for Meta/TikTok feed)

```
DTC product 15-second conversion ad. 5-framework stack. 9:16 vertical for
mobile feed.

ACT 1 — Attention (0-2s):
Hero product shot on clean white backdrop. Three-point studio lighting.
Slight orbit right 10° revealing optimal angle. Bold text overlay at 0.5s:
"[PROBLEM STATEMENT as question]". Product centered on rule-of-thirds.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: AIDA Attention. Pattern Interruption. Product recognition
sets up Anchoring (premium product presentation).

ACT 2 — Interest via JTBD (2-5s):
Cut to customer context — [TARGET DEMOGRAPHIC] using [PRODUCT] in natural
environment (home, desk, outdoors). Hand or body visible. Natural daylight.
Clean grade. Subtle micro-action showing product doing its job.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: JTBD — viewer sees product performing the customer's
job. Mirror Neurons engage on hand/body action. Viewer mentally substitutes
themselves.

ACT 3 — Desire via Stack (5-12s):
  - 5-7s: benefit statistic hero. "[PRODUCT] saves [X] [UNIT] per [TIME]"
    in bold type overlay. This is the anchor.
  - 7-10s: PEAK MOMENT — slow-motion reaction shot. Customer satisfied
    expression. Close-up on face. 3-second hold.
  - 10-12s: micro social proof — subtle text overlay "12,847 reviews ★★★★★"
    or "Sold out twice this month."
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Anchoring (stat establishes value reference). Peak-End
Rule engineering (reaction moment = peak). Social Proof (review count
triggers conformity). Desire compounds.

ACT 4 — Action via Urgency (12-15s):
Final frame: product hero returns. Price reveal — original "~~$149~~"
strikethrough at 12.3s, "NOW $79" at 12.7s in accent color. Scarcity:
"47 left" bottom-left. CTA button: "[BUY NOW]" in accent color, bottom-
center. URL persistent. Brand logo top-left. Frame holds 2.5 seconds.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Anchoring complete (original → discount shown).
Loss Aversion (save $70 = loss if you don't buy). Scarcity (47 left).
Hick's Law protected (single CTA). Conversion activated.
```

### Template 2 — Amazon listing video (30s) — optimized for consideration + add-to-cart

```
Amazon listing video, 30 seconds, 16:9 aspect. Buyer-intent audience
(already on listing page).

ACT 1 — Attention (0-3s):
Product hero on clean white. Turntable slow orbit (see style 09). Brand
logo.

ACT 2 — JTBD Demo (3-15s):

  - Use case 1: [most common customer job]
  - Use case 2: [secondary customer job]

  - 18-22s: benefit list (3 bullets max) with Loss Aversion framing

ACT 4 — Action (25-30s):
"Order today — arrives Wednesday" CTA (Prime-specific urgency). Product
hero returns with Buy Box price. Brand mark.
```

## Anti-Patterns (Conversion Killers)

| Anti-pattern | Breaks |
|---|---|
| Feature list instead of JTBD | Breaks Interest act — viewer disengages |
| Price shown first (no anchor) | Breaks Anchoring — wrong reference |
| Multiple CTAs | Breaks Hick's Law |
| No scarcity / urgency | Removes Loss Aversion lever |
| No social proof | Removes Social Proof lever |
| No peak moment | Breaks Peak-End — video forgotten |
| Generic stock footage | Breaks Mirror Neurons (viewer can't identify) |
| Product absent from key frames | Breaks Anchoring + weakens memory |
| CTA doesn't match landing page | Breaks conversion path |
| Product shown without customer context | Breaks JTBD |
