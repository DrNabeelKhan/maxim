## Primary Behavioral Framework

### Sensory Anchoring

Food imagery triggers sensory memory (taste, smell, temperature) via cross-modal perception. Brain registers food video visually but PRODUCES sensory response physiologically.

**Mechanism:**
- Visual cortex → sensory memory systems (gustatory, olfactory, thermal)
- Steam rising = warmth sensation triggered
- Sizzle sound = temperature + flavor anticipation
- Ice clink = refreshment + thermal response
- Specific cues activate specific sensory memories (coffee → morning routine memory chain; steak → luxury/celebration memory)

### Mirror Neuron Theory

Watching someone eat triggers viewer's own gustatory neurons. Close-up of fork lifting food, slow bite sequence, visible enjoyment — all produce physiological response (salivation, appetite increase).

**Application:**
- Eating sequences (not just plated food) trigger Mirror Neurons strongest
- POV "your hand" framing amplifies mirror response
- Sound of eating (bite sound, sip sound) reinforces cross-modal binding

### ASMR Neural Pathway

Specific sounds (sizzle, pour, cut, chop, crunch) trigger autonomous sensory meridian response in 30-40% of viewers. ASMR-aware food content has 3-5× higher engagement.

**Application:**
- Sound design is 50% of food video impact
- Sizzle, pour, cut, chop, crunch, pop, splash — each triggers specific neural pathway
- Isolated audio (not music-masked) activates ASMR
- Binaural-adjacent recording for high-end applications

### Combined mechanism

Sensory Anchoring creates the cognitive expectation of taste. Mirror Neurons trigger the physiological response. ASMR Neural Pathway deepens the response through cross-modal audio binding. Viewer's body literally craves the food shown — and their brain associates that craving with the featured brand/restaurant/product.

## The 5-Beat Food Film Structure

### 1. Ingredient Hero (0-3s) — activates Sensory Anchoring primer
Raw ingredient or prep material in macro. Texture, color, freshness.

### 2. Process Moment (3-7s) — activates ASMR + Mirror Neurons + thermal Sensory Anchoring
Cooking action. Sizzle, pour, cut, chop. Isolated sound.

### 3. Transformation (7-12s) — activates Sensory Anchoring deepening
Something becomes something else. Raw → cooked. Cold → hot. Craft signal.

### 4. Plating Reveal (12-18s) — activates Peak-End peak + Color-Appetite
Finished dish. Hero angle. Steam/condensation. Warm grade.

### 5. Consumption (18-25s) — activates Mirror Neurons peak + purchase trigger
Fork lift, bite, sip, pour into mouth. Satisfied reaction. PURCHASE ACTIVATION.

ACT 3 — Transformation (7-12s):

BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Sensory Anchoring deep layer. Thermal + olfactory
memory activated via visual cues alone.

ACT 4 — Plating Reveal (12-18s):
Top-down shot, chef's hands plate dish element by element. Each placement
deliberate. Final garnish drizzle from above at 16s. Audio: subtle plating
sounds.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Peak-End Rule peak. Color-Appetite maximum (warm
grade, appetizing composition).

ACT 5 — Purchase Trigger (18-20s):
45° hero angle, plated dish, steam rising, warm rim light. Restaurant

BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Brand-craving association locks in. Purchase path
activated at peak physiological craving state.

## Anti-Patterns (Craving Killers)

| Anti-pattern | Breaks |
|---|---|
| Cool color grade on hot food | Breaks Color-Appetite (unappetizing) |
| No sound / music masking | Breaks ASMR Neural Pathway |
| Too-fast pacing (cut every 1s) | Breaks Sensory Anchoring (no time to register) |
| Unrealistic portions / staging | Breaks authenticity → reduces Mirror Neuron response |
| Missing transformation beat | Finished-food only underperforms |
| Ignoring brand context | Luxury vs. fast-casual need different treatments |
| Non-isolated audio | Breaks ASMR (needs isolation from music) |
| No consumption moment | Breaks Mirror Neurons peak |
