## Primary Behavioral Framework

### Spatial Cognition

Human spatial understanding requires 3-5 seconds minimum to process a room. Cuts faster than this create disorientation rather than appreciation. Real estate video pacing must respect spatial processing limits.

**Mechanism:**
- Viewer builds mental floor plan as they watch
- Faster-than-processing pacing = viewer's mental model collapses = disengagement
- Well-paced walkthrough = viewer leaves with accurate spatial memory
- Accurate spatial memory = confidence to schedule showing

### Endowment Effect via Mental Walk-Through

Viewers who mentally "walk through" a property begin developing pre-ownership sensation. First-person POV + unhurried walk-through + lifestyle context cues accelerate this.

**Application:**
- Gimbal walk-in camera (first-person height, not drone)
- Walking pace (not racing)
- Lifestyle detail cues in frame (coffee cup on counter, book on couch, throw on bed)
- Room-to-room transitions via architectural features (NOT cuts)
- Viewer starts thinking "when I live here..." — pre-ownership locked in

### Combined mechanism

Spatial Cognition ensures the viewer's mental model of the property forms correctly. Endowment Effect via Mental Walk-Through accelerates pre-ownership feeling. Combined: viewer leaves feeling they've visited the property + starts imagining themselves in it. That emotional state drives showing requests and accelerates offers.

## Supporting Frameworks

- **Anchoring Effect**: golden-hour or blue-hour exterior establishes premium value anchor

- **Aspirational Self**: lifestyle detail cues trigger ideal-self projection ("this is who I'd be here")

## The 4-Part Property Walkthrough Structure

### 1. Establishing Exterior (0-5s) — activates Spatial Cognition macro + Anchoring
Aerial or wide exterior. Context: neighborhood, approach. Scale and value set.

### 2. Entry Transition (5-10s) — activates Mental Walk-Through onset
Approach and enter. Threshold moment. Door opens, reveals interior. Critical sensory shift.

### 3. Interior Walkthrough (10-35s) — activates Endowment Effect + Spatial Cognition micro
Room by room. Slow pacing. Hero rooms highlighted. Transitions via architectural features. Lifestyle cues throughout.

### 4. Exterior Close + Call (35-45s+) — activates Peak-End + Scarcity
Return outdoors. Listing details. Agent contact. Scarcity indicator.

## Sales / Lead-Gen Templates

### Template 1 — Residential listing 60s (optimizing for showing requests + offers)

```
Residential listing, 60 seconds, modern architectural property, natural
daylight dominant, cinematic 16:9.

ACT 1 — Exterior Establishing (0-8s):
Aerial drone opens high above property. Slowly descending and pushing in
from 45° angle. Reveals full lot, landscaping, architectural silhouette.
Settles at ground level, front facade framed with one-point perspective.
Hold 2 seconds. Light: golden hour.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Spatial Cognition macro-orientation (neighborhood
context + property scale). Anchoring (golden hour = premium + aesthetic
quality). Aspirational Self primes (the kind of place you arrive to).

ACT 2 — Entry Transition (8-14s):
Gimbal walk-in toward front door. Door opens inward as camera threshold-
crosses. Transition from exterior light to interior light — camera briefly
adjusts exposure. Enter into foyer.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Mental Walk-Through onset. First-person perspective
begins. Viewer enters the space cognitively.

ACT 3 — Interior Walkthrough (14-50s):
  Foyer to great room (14-22s): slow forward dolly through foyer. Reveals
  main living space at 18s. Hold 4 seconds on hero angle.
  Kitchen reveal (22-32s): transition through doorway (no cut). Kitchen
  with island as hero subject. 45° angle, steady Steadicam arc around
  island. 10-second continuous shot. LIFESTYLE CUE: half-full coffee pot
  on counter, fresh produce in bowl.
  Primary bedroom (32-40s): match-cut to bedroom doorway. Push-in reveals
  bed, window view through bed. Hold 4s on vista. LIFESTYLE CUE: throw
  draped on bed corner, book on nightstand.
  Bath (40-45s): transition to ensuite. Overhead reveals spa-like fixtures.
  Warm rim light.
  Outdoor space (45-50s): sliding door opens, camera follows through to
  patio/backyard. Architectural indoor-outdoor flow revealed.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Endowment Effect builds via each room. Spatial
Cognition maintained through architectural transitions (not cuts).
Lifestyle cues trigger Aspirational Self at each space. Viewer has walked
through the property mentally.

ACT 4 — Exterior Close + CTA (50-60s):
Drone ascends slowly from backyard. Reveals context again, now with sunset
lighting. Listing details overlay: "[ADDRESS] · [BEDS]BR/[BATHS]BA ·
[SQFT] sqft · $[PRICE]". Agent name + contact. Scarcity: "Private showings
only — [CONTACT]". Fade to realty logo.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Peak-End Rule end (sunset return = resonant image).
Mental-model closure (exterior contextualizes everything seen). Anchoring
reinforced (price visible within premium treatment). Scarcity activated.
Showing-request path activated at peak Endowment state.
```

## Anti-Patterns (Offer Killers)

| Anti-pattern | Breaks |
|---|---|
| Rapid cutting (<3s/space) | Breaks Spatial Cognition |
| Missing establishing shot | Breaks macro-orientation |
| Noon harsh sun | Breaks Anchoring (unflattering lighting) |
| Handheld shake | Breaks professional signal |
| Hard cuts between rooms | Breaks Mental Walk-Through + Spatial continuity |
| Aerial-only (no interior POV) | No Endowment Effect building |
| Empty staged spaces | No Aspirational Self cues |
| Too-perfect staging | Triggers "this isn't real" detection |
| Missing lifestyle cues | Weakens Aspirational Self projection |
| Price hidden | Breaks Anchoring trust |
