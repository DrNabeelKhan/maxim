## Primary Behavioral Framework

### Arousal-Based Persuasion (Petty & Cacioppo, Elaboration Likelihood Model)

High arousal reduces central-route processing (careful evaluation) and increases peripheral-route processing (emotional + cue-based). For categories where brand preference is emotional rather than analytical, this is a feature not a bug.

**Mechanism:**
- Action content produces physiological arousal (elevated heart rate, skin conductance, attention focus)
- Arousal narrows cognitive processing to emotional/cue-based
- Brand presence during arousal = brand encoded as emotionally charged
- Post-arousal, viewer remembers the feeling; brand is the anchor

### Mirror Neurons

Watching physical action activates the viewer's own motor neurons — they mentally execute the movement.

**Application:**
- Combat shots show clear body mechanics viewer can mirror
- Impact moments trigger viewer's own wince-response
- Athletic achievement shots trigger viewer's own effort-memory
- Brand preference transfers via "this brand is in the same physical state I was just in"

### Combined mechanism

Arousal-Based Persuasion produces the physiological state. Mirror Neurons make the state feel embodied (not abstract). The viewer leaves with a compressed memory: "I felt powerful/fast/strong and [BRAND] was there." That encoding drives preference.

## Supporting Frameworks

- **Kinetic Energy Perception**: brain tracks motion vectors unconsciously; correct impact-frame + motion-blur combination produces visceral response
- **Arousal-Misattribution (Dutton & Aron, 1974)**: physiological arousal from one source (action video) gets misattributed to proximate stimulus (brand presented during arousal)
- **Dopamine Anticipation**: build-up-then-release pattern (slow approach → sudden action) produces dopamine spike on release

## Behavioral → Cinematic Translation

| Action choice | Behavioral mechanism activated |
|---|---|
| Slow tension-build opening (breathing, eye lock) | Dopamine Anticipation — primes the release |
| First-strike explosion | Arousal spike — cortisol + adrenaline response |
| Impact freeze frame + chromatic flash | Kinetic Energy Perception peak encoding |
| Speed ramp (24→120fps slow-motion) | Attentional amplification during peak |
| Rapid multi-angle cuts | Sustains arousal via novelty injection |
| Clear body mechanics | Mirror Neuron activation (viewer mentally executes) |
| Sound peaks synchronized to visual | Arousal amplification via cross-modal binding |
| Brand presence during arousal peak | Arousal-Misattribution (brand = arousal source in memory) |

## The 4-Act Structure (framework-driven)

### Act 1 — Tension Build (0-2s) — activates Dopamine Anticipation
Slow approach, breathing, eye lock, single practical light source.

### Act 2 — First Strike (2-4s) — activates Arousal Peak + Mirror Neurons
Explosive initial action. Impact frame with chromatic flash. Viewer's body responds.

### Act 3 — Exchange (4-8s) — activates sustained Arousal + Kinetic Perception
Multi-angle combat exchange. Novelty sustains arousal. Clear body mechanics for Mirror Neuron continuity.

### Act 4 — Resolution Freeze (8-10s) — activates Arousal-Misattribution
Decisive final strike. Slow-motion. Brand lock-up WITHIN the arousal state.

## Sales / Lead-Gen Templates

### Template 1 — Performance sports brand (10s) — optimizing for purchase lift

```
Performance sports brand action film, 10 seconds. Tension → arousal →
arousal-misattribution structure.

ACT 1 — Tension (0-2s):
Slow medium close-up on [ATHLETE], breathing visible in cold air, eyes lock
on off-screen target. Subtle handheld micro-movement. Single practical light
source. Ambient breath-audio only — cut the music.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Dopamine Anticipation — brain primes for release.
Attention focused. Arousal baseline drops, creating contrast for upcoming
spike.

ACT 2 — First Strike (2-4s):
Explosive forward action — sprint, leap, launch. Whip-pan right following
motion. Speed ramp 24→60fps. Impact at 3.2s: freeze frame 3 frames,
chromatic flash, dust burst, SOUND peaks synchronously. Cut to wide at 3.3s
resuming 60fps.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Arousal spike — cortisol/adrenaline release within
viewer. Mirror Neurons activate — viewer mentally executes the leap/sprint.
Impact freeze produces Kinetic Energy Perception peak.

ACT 3 — Exchange / Performance Display (4-8s):
Rapid 4-cut sequence. Angles rotate clockwise around athlete. Each cut 0.8s.
Motion blur on every action. Cut 2: reverse shot (result of action). Cut 3:
medium wide (form/technique). Cut 4: low angle (impact/ground). Product
(shoe, gear, apparel) visible in multiple cuts as natural element.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Sustained arousal via novelty. Mirror Neurons continue
engaging with body mechanics. Product enters during arousal — preparing
Arousal-Misattribution encoding.

ACT 4 — Resolution + Brand (8-10s):
Decisive final action, speed ramp to 24fps slow-motion at 9.2s. Impact
freeze at 9.5s on athlete's face mid-exertion. Brand wordmark + product
hero overlay at 9.8s. Hard cut to black at 10s.
BEHAVIORAL FUNCTION: Arousal-Misattribution completes — brand encoded as
the source of the arousal state. Post-video memory: "I felt [powerful/fast]
and [BRAND] made me feel it."
```

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Behavioral mechanism it breaks |
|---|---|
| Constant action from 0s | No tension = no Dopamine Anticipation = weaker arousal peak |
| No impact freeze frames | Breaks Kinetic Energy Perception encoding |
| Unclear body mechanics (blurry, abstract) | Breaks Mirror Neuron activation |
| Brand absent during arousal peak | Breaks Arousal-Misattribution (brand doesn't get encoded) |
| Music not synchronized with impact | Breaks cross-modal binding |
| Resolution cuts to calm before brand | Arousal fades before brand lock-up encodes |
| Slow-motion on EVERYTHING | Slow-motion depends on contrast with real-time; constant slo-mo dilutes |
