# Funnel Frameworks — Quick Reference

> Shared resource for all marketing sub-skills. Reference when designing strategy, audits, or reports.

## AARRR (Pirate Metrics)

| Stage | Goal | Key Question | Example Metrics |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | Get users | How do people find us? | Traffic sources, CAC, signup rate |
| **Activation** | Deliver value | Do they have a great first experience? | Onboarding completion, time-to-value |
| **Retention** | Keep users | Do they come back? | D1/D7/D30 retention, DAU/MAU |
| **Revenue** | Make money | Do they pay? | Conversion rate, ARPU, LTV |
| **Referral** | Spread word | Do they tell others? | NPS, viral coefficient, referral rate |

**Best for:** Product-led growth, SaaS, consumer apps. Start by finding the weakest stage.

## AIDA (Attention - Interest - Desire - Action)

| Stage | Goal | Tactics |
|---|---|---|
| **Attention** | Capture notice | Headlines, ads, pattern interrupts |
| **Interest** | Engage curiosity | Problem description, storytelling |
| **Desire** | Build want | Benefits, social proof, transformation |
| **Action** | Drive conversion | CTA, urgency, risk reversal |

**Best for:** Sales pages, ads, email copy. Linear persuasion sequence.

## Flywheel (HubSpot Model)

Three interconnected stages that feed each other:

1. **Attract** -- Draw strangers in with valuable content (blog, SEO, social)
2. **Engage** -- Build relationships through personalized experiences (email, chat, CRM)
3. **Delight** -- Serve customers so well they become promoters (support, success, community)

**Best for:** Inbound-heavy businesses. Emphasizes customer delight as a growth driver, not just top-of-funnel.

## Hook Model (Nir Eyal)

| Phase | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Trigger** | External (notification) → Internal (emotion) | Push notification → boredom |
| **Action** | Simplest behavior to get reward | Open app, scroll feed |
| **Variable Reward** | Unpredictable payoff (social, hunt, self) | New content, likes, progress |
| **Investment** | User puts something in | Data, preferences, connections |

**Best for:** Engagement and retention design. Building habitual usage.

## RACE (Reach - Act - Convert - Engage)

| Stage | Digital Focus | Metrics |
|---|---|---|
| **Reach** | Awareness via owned, earned, paid media | Unique visitors, reach, impressions |
| **Act** | Interaction on site or social | Bounce rate, pages/session, social engagement |
| **Convert** | Achieve marketing goal | Leads, sales, subscribers |
| **Engage** | Long-term relationship | Repeat purchases, advocacy, LTV |

**Best for:** Digital marketing planning. Maps well to channel-specific KPIs.

## STP (Segmentation - Targeting - Positioning)

1. **Segment** the market by demographics, psychographics, behavior, needs
2. **Target** the most attractive segment(s) based on size, growth, fit
3. **Position** your offer uniquely for the target segment

**Best for:** Market entry strategy, new product positioning, expansion planning.

## Bow-Tie Funnel

Extends the traditional funnel beyond conversion:

```
Awareness → Consideration → Conversion | Retention → Expansion → Advocacy
            (traditional funnel)       |        (post-sale funnel)
```

**Best for:** SaaS and subscription businesses where post-sale revenue (upsell, cross-sell, referral) exceeds initial sale value.

## When to Use Which

| Scenario | Recommended Framework |
|---|---|
| Building a growth strategy from scratch | AARRR |
| Writing a sales page or ad | AIDA |
| Designing an inbound content program | Flywheel |
| Improving product engagement/stickiness | Hook Model |
| Planning a digital marketing campaign | RACE |
| Entering a new market segment | STP |
| Optimizing full customer lifecycle (SaaS) | Bow-Tie Funnel |
