# AI Tells — Banned Language

> Agents MUST NOT use any phrase or pattern below.
> Scan final output and rewrite any sentence containing these before delivery.
>
> This is the most operationally important file in `.brand-foundation/`. It stops
> generated content from sounding generic. Calibrated against

## Why these rules exist

Generic AI filler kills two things: authority and rhythm. Authority drops because the reader recognizes the pattern and stops trusting the source. Rhythm collapses because the banned phrases are almost always three or four syllables of empty air where a single sharp word belongs. Removing them is not a style preference. It is mechanical precision.

Banned vocabulary is banned because it undermines the voice, not because the words are wrong in isolation.

---

## Em-dash prohibition (MANDATORY, all voices)

Em dashes ( — ) fracture rhythm, dilute inevitability, and introduce visual noise. Prohibited in all Dr. Nabeel writing. Replacement protocol:

| When you would use an em-dash | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Sharp pivot or principle | **Period.** "Governance is not compliance. It is coherence." |
| Revelation after setup | **Colon.** "Alignment is not negotiation: it is resonance." |
| Linked independent truths | **Semicolon.** "Identity shapes behavior; behavior shapes destiny." |
| Pause for gravity (Philosophical voice) | **Line break** |
| Subordinate clause | **Subordinating conjunction** (because, which, that, while, whereas) |

**Exception:** em-dashes permitted inside code blocks, CLI flags, file paths, and quoted technical output where they carry semantic meaning. Never in prose.

## Generic AI Filler Words (BANNED)

- delve, dive deep, navigate, landscape, realm, unleash
- leverage (verb), robust, scalable (as lone adjective), streamline
- holistic, synergy, paradigm, game-changer, game-changing
- supercharge, transform (overused), revolutionize, disrupt (overused)
- cutting-edge, bleeding-edge, state-of-the-art, best-in-class
- innovative solution, powerful tool, comprehensive platform
- seamless, seamlessly (overused; reserve for literal integration descriptions)
- ensure, ensures (overused; prefer specific verbs)

**Why banned:** every one of these is an AI signature. Readers spot them in under two seconds. Outputs tagged 🟢 must read as if an experienced practitioner typed them at 2am, not as if a model generated them.

---

## Undermining Minimizers (BANNED)

- basically (diminishes complexity)
- just (especially "just deploy this," "just use X")
- simply (patronizes)
- obviously (assumes consensus; if it were obvious, it would not need saying)
- clearly (defensive hedge)

**Why banned:** these words make the writer sound either condescending or unsure. In the Philosophical voice they destroy inevitability. In the Friendly voice they signal that the writer does not respect the reader's intelligence.

---

## Hedging Phrases That Signal Uncertainty (BANNED unless genuinely uncertain)

- "It's worth noting that..."
- "You may want to consider..."
- "It's important to remember..."
- "Keep in mind that..."
- "One thing to note..."
- "Needless to say..." (if it is needless, cut it)
- "I think," "I feel like," "kind of," "sort of"
- "probably," "maybe," "perhaps," "might" (outside genuine uncertainty)

**Why banned:** every hedge costs the reader trust. If a claim is certain, state it. If it is uncertain, name the specific unknown and stop hiding behind fog.

---

## AI Structural Clichés (BANNED)

- "No X. No Y. Just Z." constructions
- "It's not just about X. It's about Y."
- "Enter: [thing]"
- "And here's the kicker"
- "X changed everything"
- "The best part?"
- "Want access?"
- "If you're serious about X, [CTA]"
- "To your success" sign-offs
- "Not because of X. But because of Y."
- "The future of X is here"
- "In a world where..."
- "Picture this:"
- "Let that sink in."

**Why banned:** these are the structural tells of LLM-generated copy. One cliché kills the whole piece. The reader stops reading the sentence and starts categorizing the source.

---

## Filler Adverbs (BANNED as filler)

- just, very, actually, basically, simply, truly, really, quite
- somewhat, rather, fairly, incredibly, extremely

**Rule:** if the sentence still means the same thing without the adverb, the adverb was filler. Delete it. If the intensity matters, use a stronger verb or a specific number.

---

## Banned Openers (BANNED)

- "In today's digital landscape..."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "As an AI language model..."
- "Great question!"
- "Certainly!" / "Absolutely!" / "Of course!"
- "I'd be happy to help..."
- "Let me break this down for you..."
- "Here's everything you need to know about..."

**Why banned:** openers set the voice. A banned opener makes the next 500 words fight an uphill battle to recover authority.

---

## Banned Punctuation Patterns

- Em-dashes in prose (see mandatory protocol above)
- Three or more exclamation marks per piece
- Arrow (→) in bullet lists; use hyphens `-`
- Asterisks (`*`) as bullet markers; use hyphens `-`
- Ellipsis (`...`) for suspense; only permitted in truncated quotes
- Double-spaces between sentences
- ALL CAPS for emphasis (except acronyms)

---

## Corporate Buzzwords (BANNED unless defining a specific mechanism)

- synergy (only if naming the specific mechanism)
- ecosystem (only if describing actual system relationships)
- leverage as a verb (as a noun, fine)
- disrupt (reserve for actual market shifts)
- transform (overused; use specific verb)
- optimize (only with a named metric)
- enable (overused; use specific verb)
- empower (especially "empower users"; vague)
- unlock (overused)

---

## Motivational Clichés (BANNED)

- crush it, hustle, grind, 10x (unless mathematical), game-changer
- rockstar, ninja (as role descriptors)
- "give 110 percent"
- "level up" (overused)

**Why banned:** they signal startup-bro culture, not hybrid-strategist authority.

---

## Words That Trigger Audit Even If Used Once

These are not banned outright but require a citation, audit reference, or proof. If used without support, they trigger a compliance review before publication.

- "guarantee" / "guaranteed" (legal exposure)
- "best" / "fastest" / "cheapest" (false superlatives without proof)
- "first ever" / "world's first" (provable claim required)
- "proven" (requires citation)
- "secure" / "safe" (requires security audit reference)
- "compliant" (requires named framework and audit date)
- "free" (requires scope of what is free and what is not)

---

## Positive vocabulary (USE these)

Balanced against the banned list. When a banned word is flagged, reach for one of these. Not every positive word fits every voice; match to the voice profile.

### Shared across all voices

- pattern, architecture, infrastructure, framework, system
- coherence, alignment, trajectory, rhythm
- precision, clarity, signal, depth
- implementation, integration, specification
- mechanism, substrate, lens
- Specific numbers, specific dates, specific citations

### Philosophical Architect only

- inevitability, sovereignty, essence, destiny
- architectural, geometric, structural
- "made visible," "at scale," "under load"

### Friendly Educator only

- "Think of it this way"
- "Here is what actually happens"
- "Let me show you"
- "The confusion stems from"
- "The real-world implication is"

### Technical Educator only

- "Specification," "requirement," "assumption"
- "shall," "must," "will" (in regulatory context)
- "version," "release," "rollback," "audit trail"

## How agents apply this file

1. Generate draft
2. Scan draft against every category above
3. For each banned hit, rewrite the sentence using the positive vocabulary or a structural alternative
4. Re-scan until clean
5. Only then tag 🟢 HIGH

A single em-dash, cliché opener, or banned filler word fails the scan. Tag 🟡 MEDIUM until the rewrite is clean.
